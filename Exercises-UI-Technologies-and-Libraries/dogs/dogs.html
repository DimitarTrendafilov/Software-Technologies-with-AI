Frequently Asked Questions

When choosing a dog breed, consider your living situation (apartment vs. house), activity level, available time for exercise and grooming, and family members' needs. Different breeds have different energy levels, sizes, and temperaments. It's important to research the breed's characteristics and ensure they align with your lifestyle.

The average lifespan of a dog is typically between 10-13 years, but this varies significantly by breed. Smaller breeds tend to live longer (12-18 years), while larger breeds have shorter lifespans (7-10 years). Proper care, nutrition, and regular veterinary checkups can help extend your dog's life.

Most dogs need 30 minutes to 2 hours of exercise per day, depending on the breed and age. High-energy breeds like Border Collies and Huskies need more exercise, while low-energy breeds like Bulldogs need less. Regular walks, playtime, and mental stimulation are all important for your dog's health and happiness.

A balanced diet with high-quality proteins, healthy fats, and essential nutrients is crucial for your dog's health. Consult with your veterinarian to determine the best diet based on your dog's age, size, activity level, and health condition. Commercial dog food, home-prepared meals, or a combination can all be appropriate depending on your dog's needs.

Grooming frequency depends on your dog's breed and coat type. Short-haired dogs may need grooming every 4-6 weeks, while long-haired breeds might need weekly grooming. Regular brushing, nail trimming, ear cleaning, and dental care are important for maintaining your dog's health and hygiene.
